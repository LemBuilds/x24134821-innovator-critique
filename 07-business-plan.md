# 07 — Business Plan & Marketing Campaign

## Executive Summary

SavannaLens is a community-powered wildlife intelligence platform that turns local knowledge in Ghana's savanna communities into conservation science and photographer income. We connect 18,000+ potential community contributors around Mole National Park with a $2.3B African wildlife photography market and a growing conservation data economy — through an offline-first mobile app, a cooperative photo marketplace, and a real-time biodiversity API.

**Seeking:** $850,000 seed round for 18-month runway to 200 contributors, first B2B data contracts, and Series A preparation.

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1 — Larabanga Launch (Months 1–6)
- **Target:** 200 contributors in Larabanga, Murugu, and Damongo
- **Channel:** Village chief endorsements, community radio (Radio Savannah 90.5 FM), in-person onboarding workshops in Dagbanli
- **Incentive:** First 200 users receive a free lens attachment kit (worth GHS 85 / ~$5.50)
- **KPIs:** 200 registered users, 1,500 verified sightings, 3 pilot institutional data buyers

### Phase 2 — Northern Ghana Expansion (Months 7–12)
- **Target:** Expand to Bui National Park and Digya National Park corridors
- **Channel:** Partnership with Ghana Wildlife Society regional offices, Vodafone Ghana zero-rating deal
- **KPIs:** 800 active contributors, first commercial photo license sold, first data API contract signed

### Phase 3 — West Africa Scale (Months 13–18)
- **Target:** Nigeria (Yankari Game Reserve), Côte d'Ivoire (Comoé), Senegal (Niokolo-Koba)
- **Channel:** WWF West Africa regional office partnership, African Wildlife Foundation co-marketing
- **KPIs:** 3,000 contributors, $200K ARR, Series A readiness

## Marketing Campaign — "The Witness Economy"

### Campaign Concept
The world's most valuable wildlife photos aren't in the archives of National Geographic. They're on the phones of farmers in Larabanga who have never been paid for what they see. SavannaLens opens a market that has always existed but never had a door.

### Campaign Pillars

**1. "First Frame" Stories** — short documentary portraits of community photographers and their first licensed image, distributed via Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and editorial partnerships (BBC Africa Eye, Al Jazeera Africa)

**2. The 78% Campaign** — a data-driven awareness campaign about the conservation data gap: "78% of Africa's wildlife goes undocumented. SavannaLens is fixing that." Distributed via LinkedIn (targeting conservation professionals, NGO fundraisers), Twitter/X science community, and academic networks

**3. Researcher Spotlight** — monthly feature where conservation researchers publicly acknowledge the community photographers whose data contributed to published papers — creating aspiration and professional recognition within communities

**4. Cooperative Fund Transparency Reports** — quarterly public reports showing exactly how the 20% cooperative fund is used (school equipment, water infrastructure, seedling programs) — builds trust and drives organic word-of-mouth
