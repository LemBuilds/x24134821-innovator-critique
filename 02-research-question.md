# 02 — Research Question

**Domain:** Conservation Science × Digital Anthropology × Development Economics

---

## Primary Research Question

To what extent do informal wildlife observation practices among rural and peri-urban communities bordering protected areas in northern Ghana contribute to biodiversity monitoring data, and what institutional, technological, and economic barriers prevent this observational knowledge from being systematically integrated into national conservation policy frameworks?

---

## Sub-questions

1. **Data gap:** How significant is the discrepancy between professional researcher-generated and community-generated wildlife sighting records in the Mole National Park buffer zone, measured by spatial coverage, temporal frequency, and species diversity?

2. **Exclusion mechanisms:** What structural factors — including literacy, land tenure, income, digital access, and language — determine whether a community member adjacent to a Ghanaian protected area participates in formal wildlife monitoring programs?

3. **Economic dimension:** Could a micro-income model attached to verified wildlife photography observations alter participation rates among smallholder farming households within 20km of Mole National Park?

4. **Policy integration:** Under what conditions would the Wildlife Division of Ghana's Forestry Commission incorporate citizen-science data generated via mobile platforms into its IUCN-reporting and biodiversity action planning processes?

---

## Academic Framing

This question sits at the intersection of:
- **Conservation biology** (citizen science, participatory monitoring)
- **Development economics** (inclusive value chains, pro-poor ecotourism)
- **Science and Technology Studies** (technology adoption in low-resource settings)
- **African studies** (community land rights, post-colonial conservation governance)

**Proposed methodology:** Mixed methods — spatial analysis of existing sighting data, semi-structured interviews with 40–60 community members, and a 6-month pilot deployment of a mobile documentation platform with impact measurement via difference-in-differences analysis.
